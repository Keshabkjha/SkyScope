# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

**Be Respectful and Considerate**

- Treat others with respect, professionalism, and kindness
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accept constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members

**Be Collaborative**

- Work together to resolve conflicts and disagreements
- Listen carefully and seek to understand different perspectives
- Help others learn and grow
- Share knowledge and experience openly
- Recognize and credit the contributions of others

**Be Professional**

- Maintain a professional and courteous demeanor
- Avoid personal attacks, trolling, or inflammatory comments
- Respect privacy and confidentiality
- Follow the project's established processes and guidelines
- Be accountable for your actions and words

### Unacceptable Behavior

**Harassment and Discrimination**

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

**Disruptive Behavior**

- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Harassing photography or recording
- Sustained disruption of discussions, talks, or other events
- Spamming, excessive off-topic communication, or unsolicited commercial content
- Knowingly making false claims or spreading misinformation

**Technical Misconduct**

- Intentionally introducing malicious code, vulnerabilities, or backdoors
- Plagiarism or copyright infringement
- Deliberate destruction of project resources or data
- Abuse of project systems, APIs, or infrastructure
- Violation of security policies or data protection regulations

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- **GitHub repositories**: Issues, pull requests, discussions, and code reviews
- **Communication channels**: Slack, Discord, email lists, and other official channels
- **Events**: Meetups, conferences, workshops, and other community gatherings
- **Social media**: Official project accounts and community-managed spaces
- **Private interactions**: When acting as a project representative

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or its community in public spaces. Examples of representing a project or community include using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by:

**Primary Contact**

- **Email**: conduct@skyscope.dev
- **GitHub Issues**: [Create a private issue](https://github.com/Keshabkjha/SkyScope/issues/new?labels=conduct&template=conduct_report.md)

**Alternative Contacts**

If you're uncomfortable reporting to the primary contacts:

- **Project Maintainer**: [Keshab Jha](mailto:keshab@example.com)
- **GitHub Support**: For platform-specific violations

### What to Include in Your Report

Please include as much information as possible:

- Your contact information (optional, but helpful for follow-up)
- The names or usernames of involved parties
- Description of the behavior that occurred
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Any relevant screenshots, logs, or other evidence
- Any witnesses to the incident

### Response Process

All reports will be reviewed and investigated by the project maintainers and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

**Timeline**

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24-48 hours
- **Initial Assessment**: Within 3-5 business days
- **Resolution**: As soon as reasonably possible, typically within 2 weeks

**Possible Actions**

- **Private Warning**: For first-time or minor violations
- **Temporary Suspension**: For repeated or serious violations
- **Permanent Ban**: For severe or repeated violations
- **Removal of Content**: For inappropriate posts or comments
- **Required Training**: For educational purposes

### Appeals

If you believe a consequence is unjust or disproportionate, you may appeal by:

1. Contacting a different project maintainer
2. Providing additional context or evidence
3. Requesting a review by the project leadership team

The appeal will be reviewed within 5 business days, and a final decision will be communicated to all parties involved.

## Consequences

Participants who do not follow this Code of Conduct may be subject to:

### Temporary Measures

- **Warning**: Private or public warning about behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Limited access to community resources (1-30 days)
- **Content Removal**: Deletion of inappropriate content
- **Required Training**: Completion of relevant educational materials

### Permanent Measures

- **Permanent Ban**: Complete removal from all community spaces
- **Legal Action**: For violations involving illegal activities
- **Public Statement**: Disclosure of serious violations to protect the community

### Restoration

Participants who have been temporarily suspended may request restoration of access by:

1. Acknowledging the violation
2. Demonstrating understanding of the Code of Conduct
3. Committing to future compliance
4. Completing any required training or remediation
